Earthquake Safety Guide

What is Earthquake?

An earthquake is the sudden shaking or vibration of the Earth's surface, caused by a rapid release of energy from within the Earth's crust. This energy release creates seismic waves that move through the Earth, causing the ground to shake. Earthquakes can range from mild tremors to violent movements that cause serious damage.

Causes of Earthquakes

  1. Tectonic Plate Movements (Main Cause)
    • The Earth's crust is made up of large plates called tectonic plates. When these plates move, they can collide, slide past each other, or pull apart.
    • The stress builds up at the boundaries (faults), and when it’s released, it causes an earthquake.
  2. Volcanic Activity
    • When magma rises during a volcanic eruption, it can cause earthquakes near the volcano. These are called volcanic earthquakes.
  3. Human Activities (Induced Earthquakes)
    • Activities like mining, drilling, dam construction, and underground explosions can trigger small to moderate earthquakes.
  4. Fault Slipping (Strike-Slip, Normal, or Thrust Faults)
    • Sudden movement along a fault line (a crack in the Earth’s crust) releases energy, causing earthquakes.
  5. Isostatic Rebound
    • In areas where glaciers have melted, the Earth's crust slowly rises back up, which can also trigger small earthquakes.

Before

Secure heavy furniture and appliances to walls. Prepare an emergency kit stocked with water, food, flashlight, and first aid. Identify safe spots at home such as under sturdy tables, and create an evacuation plan with family.

During

Drop, cover, and hold on! Stay indoors away from windows and glass. Take shelter under sturdy furniture or cover your head and neck with your arms. If outside, move to an open area away from buildings.

After

Check yourself and others for injuries. Be prepared for aftershocks. Avoid damaged structures and stay updated through official channels. Help neighbors if you can and report hazards.
