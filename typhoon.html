Typhoon Preparedness

A typhoon is a mature tropical cyclone that develops in the western part of the North Pacific Ocean. It can bring strong winds, heavy rains, and flooding. Preparation and early warnings are key to safety.

What to Do Before a Typhoon

  • Secure your home: Check your roof, windows, and doors.
  • Stock up on emergency supplies: water, food, medicine, flashlight, batteries.
  • Stay updated: Monitor weather forecasts and alerts from authorities.
  • Evacuate if needed: Know your evacuation center and route.

During a Typhoon

  • Stay indoors and away from windows.
  • Turn off gas and electricity if flooding occurs.
  • Keep emergency contacts handy.

After a Typhoon

  • Check for structural damage and hazards before going outside.
  • Avoid floodwaters; they may be contaminated or electrically charged.
  • Report emergencies and assist others if safe to do so.
