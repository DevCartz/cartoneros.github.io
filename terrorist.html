Earthquake Safety Guide

What is an Terrorist Attack?

A terrorist attack disaster is a violent act intended to cause fear, harm, or destruction, usually targeting civilians, public places, or government infrastructure. These attacks are often politically, ideologically, or religiously motivated and can involve bombings, shootings, hijackings, or cyber-attacks. The consequences can be severe—loss of life, trauma, destruction, and long-term psychological and social impact.

Before

  1. Intelligence gathering and surveillance to detect threats.
  2. Security checks and screenings at airports, public events, and buildings.
  3. Public awareness programs on how to report suspicious activity.
  4. Training for police and emergency responders in anti-terror procedures.
  5. Cybersecurity measures to protect data and critical systems.
  6. Evacuation plans and drills in schools, offices, and public places.

During

  1. Stay calm and seek safety—hide, run, or escape if possible.
  2. Follow instructions from security personnel or authorities.
  3. Call emergency services (if safe to do so) and report details.
  4. Avoid crowded exits that could become dangerous bottlenecks.
  5. Help others only if you can do so without putting yourself at risk.
  6. Do not spread misinformation on social media during unfolding events.

After

  1. Medical treatment for the injured and psychological support for survivors.
  2. Rescue and recovery operations by trained emergency teams.
  3. Investigation to find out who was responsible and how it happened.
  4. Security review and strengthening of vulnerable areas.
  5. Compensation and aid for victims and families.
  6. Community healing programs to reduce fear, promote unity, and build resilience.
