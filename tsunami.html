Tsunami Safety Guide

What is Tsunami?

A tsunami is a series of large, powerful ocean waves usually caused by underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides. When these waves reach coastal areas, they can flood land, destroy buildings, and cause massive loss of life and property. A tsunami disaster occurs when a tsunami strikes a populated area with little or no warning.

Before

  1. Early warning systems using seismic and ocean sensors.
  2. Education and drills for communities in coastal areas.
  3. Evacuation routes and signs clearly marked.
  4. Avoid building homes and critical infrastructure too close to the shore.
  5. Community awareness programs about natural tsunami signs (e.g., sudden sea retreat).
  6. Emergency kits and plans for families and schools.

During

  1. Evacuate immediately to higher ground if you feel a strong earthquake or see the sea suddenly recede.
  2. Follow official alerts and warnings from sirens, radio, or SMS.
  3. Do not go near the shore to watch waves—more may follow.
  4. Help others evacuate if you can do so safely.
  5. Stay in safe zones until authorities say it’s safe to return.

After

  1. Search and rescue operations to find survivors.
  2. Medical aid and shelter for injured and displaced people.
  3. Clear debris and restore essential services (water, electricity, communication).
  4. Assess environmental and structural damage.
  5. Provide emotional and financial support to affected families.
  6. Rebuild with stronger infrastructure and update disaster plans.
